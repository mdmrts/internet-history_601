HOW THE WORLD CHANGE THE WEB

YouTube is a video-sharing platform online video-sharing platform founded by three former PayPal employees Chad Hurley, Steve Chen, Jawed Karim in February 2005. Google bought the site in November 2006. Digg is a social network and news aggregating site founded by Kevin Rose and Jay Adelson in 2004. Users submit stories for promotion, and they are subsequently either voted for (digged) or against (buried). Facebook, offering online social networking services. Facebook was founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg, Eduardo Saverin, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes, all of whom were students at Harvard University.

NAPSTER was founded by Shawn Fanning and Sean Parker. Initially, Napster was envisioned by Fanning as an independent peer-to-peer file sharing service. The service operated between June 1999 and July 2001. Its technology allowed people to easily share their MP3files with other participants. Winamp is a media player for Microsoft Windows originally developed by Justin Frankel and Dmitry Boldyrev by their company Nullsoft. Since version 2 it has been sold as freemium and supports extensibility with plug-ins and skins, and features music visualization, playlist and a media library, supported by a large online community.

Wikipedia is a free content, multilingual online encyclopedia written and maintained by a community of volunteers through a model of open collaboration, using a wiki-based editing system. It was launched on January 15, 2001, by its creators Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger. Craigslist (stylized as craigslist) is a classified advertisements website with sections devoted to jobs, housing, for sale, items wanted, services, community service, gigs, résumés, and discussion forums. It’s founded by Craig Newmark in 1995.
